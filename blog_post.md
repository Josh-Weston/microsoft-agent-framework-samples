# Nova Scotia’s 2026‑27 Budget: Powering Growth, Innovation & Community Resilience

## Introduction

- **The Hook:** *$3.5 billion* in infrastructure spending and a historic *$4.4 million* boost for artificial intelligence signal a bold new chapter for Nova Scotia.  
- **The Context:** The province’s 2026‑27 budget frames a long‑term vision—defending Nova Scotia by strengthening its economy, modernising health‑care, expanding affordable housing, and investing in cutting‑edge technology.  
- **The Thesis/Value Proposition:** By the end of this post you’ll understand the key spending priorities, how they translate into everyday benefits, and why AI is emerging as a distinct pillar of the province’s growth strategy.

---

## Growing the Economy Through Clean Energy & Strategic Sectors

Nova Scotia is committing **$3.6 million** to the Green Hydrogen Action Plan and **$25 million** to the Subsurface Energy Research and Development program with Dalhousie University. These funds aim to unlock on‑shore natural gas resources responsibly while positioning the province as a leader in clean‑energy innovation.

- **Supporting Detail:** The budget allocates **$1.5 million** to Invest Nova Scotia, targeting six priority sectors—including **Artificial Intelligence and the digital economy**—to drive GDP growth.

## Strengthening Health Care & Community Well‑Being

Rebuilding the health system is a cornerstone of the budget. Over **$1.2 billion** is earmarked for the Halifax Infirmary Expansion and the Cape Breton Regional Health Care redevelopment, while **$9.6 million** funds digital tools that help citizens locate services and improve provider communication.

- **Supporting Detail:** A $5.5 million investment in the AlayaCare platform will streamline home‑care delivery, and **$4.4 million** will establish an Artificial Intelligence capability to enhance health‑care analytics.
- **Actionable Advice:** Nova Scotians can download the **YourHealthNS** app to access their digital health records, book appointments, and benefit from the new AI‑driven care pathways.

---

## Expanding Housing, Education & Social Supports

Affordable housing receives a massive **$46.4 million** to build new public units in communities from Sackville to Glace Bay, while **$30.8 million** funds post‑secondary student housing at NSCC campuses. The budget also sustains tax‑saving measures that will save the average family over **$1,400** annually.

- **Supporting Detail:** Targeted programs such as the First‑Time Homebuyers Pilot and the **$34.3 million** Skills‑Growth plan will help young adults and skilled tradespeople secure stable homes and careers.

## Artificial Intelligence Initiatives – A Separate Spotlight

Artificial intelligence (AI) is highlighted as a strategic growth engine across multiple sectors:

| AI Initiative | Funding | Expected Impact |
|---------------|--------|-----------------|
| AI‑enabled applications (provincial tech platform) | **$4.4 million** | Faster decision‑making, improved data security, and new public‑service efficiencies. |
| AI traffic‑signal pilot in Halifax | **$1 million** | Smarter traffic flow, reduced congestion, and lower emissions. |
| AI capabilities in health‑care analytics | **$4.4 million** (included above) | Enhanced disease monitoring, predictive health outcomes, and optimized resource allocation. |
| AI support for digital transformation (Enterprise Resource Planning, cybersecurity) | **$41.9 million** (ERP) + **$33.8 million** (cybersecurity) | Strengthened provincial infrastructure, safeguarding data while leveraging AI‑driven threat detection. |

- **Key Takeaway:** By carving out a dedicated AI budget, Nova Scotia is positioning itself to attract tech talent, spur innovation in traditional industries, and improve public‑service delivery.
